The Best Movies According to Siddharth

My top 3 movies of all time

1. KGF 1 and 2

KGF Chapter 1 follows the journey of Rocky, a fearless and ambitious man who rises from poverty in the streets of Mumbai to become a powerful gangster. His mission takes him to the Kolar Gold Fields (KGF), a brutal and secretive gold mine ruled by oppressive forces. Rocky infiltrates KGF to fulfill a promise to his dying mother — to become the most powerful man. The film ends with Rocky killing the tyrant Garuda, setting the stage for a new era.

KGF Chapter 2 continues Rocky’s story as he becomes the ruler of KGF. With great power comes bigger enemies — including the fierce Prime Minister Ramika Sen and ruthless crime lords like Adheera. Rocky must defend his empire while staying true to his mother’s dream. The sequel dives deeper into politics, betrayal, and power struggles, with grand action and emotional depth. It concludes with shocking twists and the legend of Rocky growing even larger.

2. Munna Bhai MBBS

Munna Bhai M.B.B.S. (2003) is a heartwarming comedy-drama about Munna, a lovable Mumbai gangster who pretends to be a doctor to fulfill his father’s dream. When the truth is exposed, he decides to actually join a medical college to prove himself. With his street-smart ways and big heart, Munna challenges the rigid and cold system of medical education. He brings laughter, empathy, and humanity to the hospital, showing that healing is more than just medicine — it’s about kindness and love.

3. 3 idiots

3 Idiots is an inspiring and emotional story about three engineering students — Rancho, Farhan, and Raju — navigating life at a strict and competitive college in India. Rancho, the most unconventional of them, questions the education system and encourages his friends to follow their passion instead of blindly chasing success. Through humor, struggle, and friendship, the film highlights the pressure students face, the flaws in the system, and the power of learning with curiosity. It ends with a powerful message: “Chase excellence, and success will follow.”
